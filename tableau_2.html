Interactive Tableau Dashboard – Duration vs. Track Popularity by Genre

Below is an embedded Tableau dashboard exploring how track duration relates to popularity, and how this relationship differs across genres. Use Tableau’s interactive filters to compare genres, highlight clusters, and examine outliers in the dataset.

Dashboard: Full Data Overview

The scatterplot visualizes each track’s duration (in milliseconds) against its popularity score. By color-coding points by genre, the dashboard makes it easy to examine genre-specific patterns and determine whether certain genres favor longer or shorter track lengths.

Key Insights

The scatterplot reveals that the vast majority of tracks fall within a relatively narrow duration range—roughly 150,000 to 300,000 milliseconds (2.5 to 5 minutes). Within this range, popularity varies widely, indicating that track length alone is not a strong predictor of popularity.

Genres cluster heavily in this core duration range as well. Pop, hip-hop, indie, and R&B tracks especially overlap, reflecting mainstream conventions around track length. While these genres show similar duration patterns, they display diverse popularity scores, suggesting that other audio features (energy, danceability, loudness, etc.) may play a larger role in determining how well a track performs.

Classical music displays a unique pattern: several classical tracks extend far beyond 400,000 ms (over 6 minutes), reaching durations of 800,000–1,100,000 ms. These long-duration pieces tend to cluster at mid-range popularity levels, suggesting niche but consistent listener engagement. A few rock and funk tracks also appear as high-duration outliers, though they are far less common.

Across all genres, no strong linear relationship emerges between duration and popularity. Popular tracks can be both short and long, and lower-popularity tracks are equally scattered. The visualization suggests that popularity is far more influenced by musical style, production, or audience expectations than by duration alone.

Interactivity

Tableau’s interactive filtering and highlighting allow you to isolate individual genres and examine their distributions more closely. Hovering over points reveals track-specific details, helping you spot genre patterns, popularity concentrations, or duration-based outliers with greater precision.

References & Notes

Built with Tableau Public, this visualization is part of a larger analysis exploring how musical attributes—such as duration, energy, loudness, and playlist categorization—relate to streaming popularity across genres.
